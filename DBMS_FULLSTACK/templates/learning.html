Essential Stock Market Terms

Understanding these key concepts will help you make more informed investment decisions. Familiarize yourself with these terms before diving into stock predictions and investments.

Trading Basics

OP

Open Price

The open price is the price at which a security first trades when the market opens for the day. It's the starting point for daily price action analysis.

Significant gaps between previous close and opening prices can indicate strong market sentiment based on overnight news or events.

CP

Close Price

The close price is the final price at which a security trades during a regular trading session. It's used as the reference point for daily performance calculations.

Many technical indicators and chart patterns use closing prices as they're considered more representative of market sentiment than intraday prices.

Valuation Metrics

P/E

P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)

The P/E ratio measures a company's current share price relative to its per-share earnings. It indicates how much investors are willing to pay for each dollar of earnings.

A high P/E could mean the stock is overvalued or investors expect high growth rates in the future. A low P/E might indicate undervaluation or concerns about the company's future.

DY

Dividend Yield

Dividend yield represents the annual dividend payment as a percentage of the stock's current price. It shows the return on investment from dividends alone.

Higher dividend yields can indicate value stocks or established companies, while lower yields might represent growth-focused businesses that reinvest earnings.

MC

Market Capitalization

Market cap is the total value of all a company's shares of stock, calculated by multiplying the share price by the number of outstanding shares.

Market cap categorizes companies as large-cap, mid-cap, or small-cap, each with different risk profiles and growth potentials.

Technical Indicators

BB

Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (usually a 20-day simple moving average) with an upper and lower band that represent price volatility. The bands widen during volatile markets and narrow during less volatile periods.

Used to identify overbought or oversold conditions and potential price reversals when the price touches or crosses the bands.

RSI

RSI (Relative Strength Index)

RSI is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale from 0 to 100. It identifies overbought (above 70) or oversold (below 30) conditions.

RSI helps traders identify potential reversal points and divergences between price and momentum.

Market Fundamentals

IR

Inflation Rate

Inflation rate measures the percentage increase in the general price level of goods and services over a period of time. It erodes purchasing power and affects interest rates.

High inflation can lead to higher interest rates and may negatively impact stock valuations, especially for growth stocks.

GDP

GDP Growth Rate

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) growth rate measures how fast an economy is growing. It's the percentage change in a country's total economic output compared to the previous period.

Strong GDP growth often correlates with healthy corporate profits and potentially positive stock market performance.
